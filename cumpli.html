LEGAL AND REGULATORY COMPLIANCE

A PRIORITY FOR MODERN BANKING

Do you know what

is the percentage of the operating budget that on average a financial entity allocates to legal and regulatory compliance?

7%-10%

Do you know which aspects are most commonly violated in terms of Legal and Regulatory Compliance?

Aspects related to Anti-Money Laundering and Data Protection

Do you know how much money banks have paid in the last decade in fines for regulatory non-compliance?

+ 300 billion dollars

In an increasingly complex financial environment, legal and regulatory compliance has become essential for the stability, reputation, and sustainability of banking institutions. Global regulations like Basel III and FATCA, along with local regulations, require banks to adopt robust policies and processes to prevent legal, operational, and reputational risks.

CHALLENGES FOR FINANCIAL ENTITIES

  • Constant increase in regulations: Technical teams must constantly update and adapt to new regulations.
  • Greater complexity: Not only is the number of laws and regulations increasing, but also their complexity, which is worsened for financial entities operating in multiple jurisdictions.
  • High operating costs: Implementing and maintaining compliance systems is expensive, especially for institutions with international operations.
  • Teams dedicated to the implementation of new regulations.
  • Greater care and effort in managing trust and reputational risk.
  • Many institutions still rely on manual processes, which increases human errors and hinders effective monitoring.
  • Internal Cultural Change: Ensuring that all staff adopt a compliance and ethical mindset can be a challenge, especially in large organizations.

Although the challenges are increasing, technology has evolved allowing banks to optimize and automate processes through RegTech technology, which together with artificial intelligence systems, can reduce the costs associated with regulatory compliance.

WHAT RECOMMENDATIONS HAS THE BASEL COMMITTEE ISSUED?

  • Establish effective compliance systems as part of internal controls.
  • The board of directors and senior management are responsible for ensuring that banks comply with applicable laws and regulations.
  • Identify and mitigate operational risks related to regulatory non-compliance.
  • Pillar 3 of Basel III sets requirements for disclosing clear and understandable information about risks and compliance.
  • Promote a culture of compliance and ethical conduct throughout the organization.
